Conclusion:

Safety and AA: Our Common Welfare,

Here are my conclusions from my research:

  1. Each group is completely separate but participants can be involved in more than one group.
  2. Each group has its own conscious. It is up to the website committee to communicate the guidelines to the webmaster and what information should be on the site.

  4. It is okay for the webmaster to be a paid position.
  5. The website committee should be the sole entity to communicate with the web master so that the web master tasks are clear and to provide resources so the web master can build and maintain the website without the added requirements of getting resources and information to build and maintain the site.

So it is my conclusion that the website committee be responsible for getting the content to the webmaster and that the web site committee handle any reimbursements to the web master if any are provided. About the Area 28 Website

From the webmaster (Last up date 12/3/19): My hope and desire for this site is to provide a place where the Trusted Servants and Service Committees can share their resources to help those who are suffering from alcoholism. It is my understanding that real recovery will come when we move through the Cyber world into real human one-on-one relationships to help each other to recover from alcoholism. Dr Bob and Bill Wilson have stated that we need each other to recover. Likewise the website needs information from you to be useful, helpful, and to stay current. When AA first started, the internet did not exist. Times are changing and with the change of time, the rules of relationships are changing. In putting this site together, we are trying to keep the 12 steps, 12 traditions, the aa.org website guidelines, and the Area 28 group conscience in focus.

Please consider doing a 12 step and send information to the web committee or the Boomerange. Those who recover well take an active part in their recovery. This means do something, not consume something<gr>.
